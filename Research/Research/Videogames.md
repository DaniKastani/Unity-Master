Videogame environments like those found in 3D action games are relatively complex, and they require players to orient themselves in a variety of locales such as buildings, city streets, and natural settings. It is conceivable that [[Navigation in Games]] - in these graphically and procedurally realistic environments - elicits the same spatial abilities (e.g., mental rotation) required in real-world navigation.
